About Me

Corin Hoke explores women’s mental health, sexuality, and the role of domesticity through her abstraction of nature and the human form. Her work aims to represent a spectrum of femininity and women, and different feelings towards sex, gender, and women’s place in our world. Femininity is something to be celebrated even though many cultures, including our own, see it as weak or lesser. Being cornered and expected to fill a traditional domestic role is suffocating. Though these institutions are not inherently bad, they have a tendency to push women’s aspirations to the back burner and take over their entire existence. Being forced into these roles by age old expectations is entrapment. Corin Hoke’s goal is to not only change the way women are viewed, but to continue an ongoing and complex conversation about femininity and gender.

Corin Hoke completed her Bachelors of Arts degree in Studio Art and English at Truman State University. She is seeking to continue her education in the arts. Her work is directly inspired by the people in her life and their observed experiences.
